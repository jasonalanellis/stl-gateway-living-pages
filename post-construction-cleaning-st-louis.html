What Post-Construction Cleaning St. Louis Actually Requires

Post-construction cleaning is not janitorial service. After construction work — drywall cutting, concrete grinding, masonry, and finish carpentry — the air and surfaces contain crystalline silica particles under 10 microns. These particles are classified by OSHA as a serious health hazard: they cause silicosis, an irreversible lung disease.

Standard shop vacs and residential vacuums recirculate these particles. The result is what the industry calls "The Settling Error" — dust visibly reappears on surfaces 24 hours after cleaning. Legitimate post-construction cleaning requires:

  • True HEPA-13 vacuum extraction — 99.97% filtration at 0.3 microns
  • Negative air containment — prevents silica migration through HVAC ductwork
  • Multi-pass protocol — extract, wait 24 hours for settlement, extract again
  • 275°F steam sanitization — for fixture surfaces and hard-to-reach areas
